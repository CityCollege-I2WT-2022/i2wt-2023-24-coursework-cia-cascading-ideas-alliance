Silence of the Lamps

Year Language Running time
1991 English 1:58:39

Rating: ★★★★★

In a genre obsessed with shadows and suspense, Silence of the Lamps flips the switch, illuminating the absurdity of its predecessors. A comedic beacon in a sea of thrillers.

-Satirical Screenings Gazette

Silence of the Lamps turns the horror genre on its head, and we can't stop laughing. The comedic timing is impeccable, and the lamp-centric plot twists are both absurd and genius. A comedic tour de force that deserves a standing ovation!

-Giggle Cinema Monthly

Move over horror, 'Silence of the Lamps' has arrived to scare you... with laughter! It's so funny, we heard even Hitchcock would have traded his suspense for a good chuckle. Who needs screams when you can have side-splitting guffaws?

-CineGiggle Monthly

Director: Stanley Lighthouser
Cast:
Clarabelle Starling Emma Witmore
Dr. Hannibal Bulb Christopher Jest
Buffalo Bulb Martin Witlaugh
Lector Le Lumière Daniel Nightlight
Mason Verre Samuel Humorson

Plot

The story revolves around Clarabelle Starling, an ambitious and somewhat naive FBI agent. When a series of lamp thefts baffles the authorities, Clarabelle is assigned to the case. She seeks the help of an unconventional, lamp-loving consultant named Dr. Hannibal Bulb, who is an eccentric character with a penchant for wordplay and a bizarre sense of humor.
As Clarabelle and Dr. Bulb delve into the world of stolen lamps, they encounter a gallery of quirky suspects, including Buffalo Bulb, a misunderstood artist; Lector Le Lumière, a flamboyant antique dealer; and Mason Verre, a wealthy collector with a shady past. The investigation takes unexpected turns as Clarabelle tries to outwit the lamp thief and solve the mystery, all while navigating the eccentricities of her peculiar consultant.
