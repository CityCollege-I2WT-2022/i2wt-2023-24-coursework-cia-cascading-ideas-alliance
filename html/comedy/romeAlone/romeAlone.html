Plot

In the bustling city of Ancient Rome during the festive season of Saturnalia, the Centurion family is preparing for a grand celebration. The head of the household, Maximus Centurion, is a high-ranking Roman official, and the family is set to attend a lavish Saturnalia feast at the emperor's palace. Amidst the chaos of packing and preparing for the celebration, young Julius Centurion, a mischievous and curious boy, is accidentally left behind as his family rushes to the palace. Unaware of his absence, the Centurion family enjoys the festivities, leaving Julius to fend for himself in the bustling streets of Rome. As the night unfolds, Julius realizes he's home alone in Ancient Rome. At first, he revels in the freedom, enjoying the opportunity to explore the city without any adult supervision. However, his excitement turns to panic as he discovers a plot by a group of misfit slaves led by the bumbling Gracchus to steal a valuable artifact from the emperor's palace. With the help of unconventional allies including a street-smart gladiator named Maximus and a resourceful vestal virgin named Flavia, Julius must outsmart the bumbling thieves, avoid Roman authorities, and navigate the treacherous streets of Ancient Rome to protect his home and family's honor. Meanwhile, Maximus Centurion and his wife, Aurelia, begin to suspect that something is amiss when they can't find Julius at the Saturnalia feast. The film cuts between Julius's hilarious escapades and the Centurion parents' increasingly frantic search through the crowded streets of Rome. In a heartwarming climax, Julius manages to thwart the thieves' plans, with the help of his newfound friends. The Centurion family is joyfully reunited, and they come to appreciate the true spirit of Saturnalia – the importance of family, friendship, and the joy of giving. "Rome Alone" delivers a humorous and heartwarming parody, blending the iconic elements of "Home Alone" with the rich historical backdrop of Ancient Rome, offering a unique and entertaining take on the beloved holiday classic.
