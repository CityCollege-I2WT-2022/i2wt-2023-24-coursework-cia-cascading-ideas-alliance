Plot

In the close-knit Greek-American community of Chicago, the Katsopolis family is known for its vibrant traditions, boisterous personalities, and, of course, its famous Greek restaurant. The patriarch, Spiros Katsopolis, has always dreamed of a lavish and unforgettable funeral that would celebrate his life in true Greek style. When Spiros unexpectedly passes away, his family, led by his spirited daughter, Maria, is determined to give him the grand send-off he always wanted. However, their plans quickly spiral out of control as cultural clashes, hilarious misunderstandings, and eccentric relatives threaten to turn the funeral into a chaotic comedy. Maria, a modern and level-headed woman, finds herself at odds with her traditional Greek family's extravagant funeral preparations. As they navigate the challenges of merging Greek customs with American sensibilities, the Katsopolis family inadvertently becomes the talk of the town. The plot thickens as long-lost relatives, eccentric neighbors, and a well-meaning but misguided funeral director get involved, turning the funeral preparations into a series of slapstick misadventures. From over-the-top floral arrangements to a dance-filled procession through the streets, the Katsopolis family brings a touch of Greek chaos to the somber affair. As the tension between tradition and modernity reaches its peak, Maria discovers her father's hidden journal, revealing that Spiros secretly wanted a simple and intimate farewell. Realizing the importance of honoring her father's true wishes, Maria must find a way to redirect the chaotic funeral preparations and bring her family together in a heartfelt celebration of Spiros's life. "My Big Fat Greek Funeral" is a hilarious and heartwarming parody that explores the clash of cultures, the absurdity of funeral traditions, and the power of family to overcome even the most challenging moments with laughter and love.
