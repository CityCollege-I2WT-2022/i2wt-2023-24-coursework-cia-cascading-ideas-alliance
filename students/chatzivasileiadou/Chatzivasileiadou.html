A few words about me

My name is Irida Chatzivasileiadou. I was born July 1st 2003. Even before I fell in love with gaming, I was extremely interested in computers, after being encourage exploring them from a young age, and decided to pursue a Computer Science education.

My first attempt at doing so was at the Department of Information and Electronic Engineering in IHU, but, due to a variety of reasons, including the grueling commute back and forth, I decided that it wasn't a match. Cue a relentless search for my next step. After looking through a variety of choices, others centered on Computer Science and others on more artistic subjects, I ended up choosing Computer Science in City College, where I reside to this day.

A few more details

As previously stated I have a deep love for gaming, I especially have a soft spot for indie puzzle games with complex puzzles and lore, but in general I enjoy most types of games. Even though indie games have a special place in my heart, the game series I've dedicated most of my time to, on and off game, is the Dark Souls series. I've played all the titles at least once, and, usually I act as a coach and lore encyclopedia for friends that want to try it.

My ultimate goal is to learn how to make realistic armors and cosplay Artorias the Abysswalker from Dark Souls.

Before high school, I also used to read a lot, but sadly the Greek education system snuffed that right out of me. About this project

I approached this project with my love for gaming and a lot of big ideas. Ideas that were probably a bit too big for what we thought we could accomplish in the timeframe we had. So we opted to do something a bit less ambitious but equally as fun and interesting. The fact that our site idea was so compelling was a big factor that made us want to work even harder, so it would come into fruition. I think our team was very lucky, because it consisted of people with very similar work ethics and ways of thinking that complemented each other.

We worked extremely close, arranging frequent meetings so we could create our website more efficiently and the work could be distributed equally, so a lot of the general templates are a group effort. My personal contributions, other than the work that was done along with my colleagues were:

  • The movie poster slideshow
  • The See More and See Less buttons
  • The star rating
  • The ads and the fake virus page they redirect to
  • And the message that pops up after the cursor is inactive
