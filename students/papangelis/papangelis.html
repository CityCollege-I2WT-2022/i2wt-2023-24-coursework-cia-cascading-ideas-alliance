A few words about me

My name is Dimitris (as mentioned at the top of the page as well in some paperwork). I was born August 23, 1993, early enough to grow up with dial-up internet, floppy disks and those phones with an antenna and the little keyboard cover.

Fast forward a couple years and I find myself employed at a company that supports a well-known cloud service (which I'm not allowed to formally disclose due to an NDA - nothing to do with NBA, the two are apparently different), when I catch myself thinking that I would like to work with computer systems in one way or another but not in customer support (extremely boring stuff).

Long story short, here I am, enrolling on the Friday just before the Induction week, embarking on this 3-year long journey. One of my better decisions I should say!

About this project

The words about our team in the About section were originally chosen for this personal website, however, as they speak for all of us, they were moved there and I am free to fill this remaining space with my final thoughts and information regarding my personal contribution to the project.

As team leader, I found myself in a position that I'm sure others would find challenging. I have to say that my case turned out in fact to be the opposite, not so much because of any skill on my part, but because I was lucky to team up with two amazing developers, who shared the work ethic, enthusiasm, attention to detail, creativity and sense of humour that came to shape our website.

We worked so much together, that outside of the parts that each one of us coded separately, it is hard to know where one contibution ends and the next starts.

As per the requirements, under my personal purview was the homepage, simple as that may be, while other personal contributions have been the final version of the navigation bar, the comment utility as well as a couple randomizer utilities for recommendations.

Finally, other contributions have been the organisation of team meetings, especially in the early days of this effort, as well as keeping minutes and creating the communication and filesharing channels that were utilized during the creation of HaHaHeaven.
